Preparing for Postpartum Testing

After gestational diabetes, new moms still need to eat healthy, stay active and take the right tests to prevent type 2 diabetes. Post-delivery care, more commonly known as postpartum care, is an essential part of preventing type 2 diabetes.

At 6 to 12 weeks postpartum, it’s required for all women with gestational diabetes to be screened for type 2 diabetes with a two-hour, 75-gram oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT).

For women who miss this exam within 13 weeks should have their HbA1c test performed.

Every year after that, women with a history of gestational diabetes should check their HbA1c to monitor for diabetes.

Droobi Fact

Women with gestational diabetes are 10 times more likely to develop type 2 diabetes in the future and this risk is not limited to within the 6-12-week post-delivery timeframe. This is why it’s so important not to miss the yearly tests!
