How to Manage Gestational Diabetes

This risk for complications can be lowered by not gaining too much weight during pregnancy and staying physically active. The main goal for diabetes self-management during pregnancy is to manage your blood sugar levels to reduce complications.

The best way to manage your diabetes to keep you and your baby healthy is:

  • Antenatal Care (care during pregnancy)

    This will be provided to you by the team of healthcare professionals and doctors. Antenatal care will include regular fetal surveillance to monitor the development and growth of the baby for any complications that may arise.

  • Healthy Eating

    The goal is to establish good nutrition, appropriate weight gain and excellent blood sugar control.

  • Physical Activity

    Doing regular exercises that are safe for you and your baby will help you gain pregnancy weight at a healthy rate, lower blood sugar, and decrease insulin resistance.

  • Self-Monitoring of Blood Glucose (SMBG)

    You should have a reliable glucometer at home for blood sugar monitoring at regular intervals.

  • Medication Management

    Taking prescribed medication as directed by your doctor. Only when diet and exercise are not enough to manage blood sugar levels will your doctor consider to prescribe medicine.

  • Postpartum Care (care after pregnancy)

    Postpartum care should include breastfeeding, testing, counseling and healthy habits to manage blood sugar and weight
