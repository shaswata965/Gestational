What is Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes is a short-lasting type of diabetes that commonly occurs in the second or third trimester, but could be also detected earlier than that.

Not all newly detected diabetes in pregnancy are gestational. Some women will discover to have type 2 diabetes, a permanent form of diabetes, at any point during pregnancy.

While gestational diabetes lasts for only a few months, the post-delivery effects can last a lifetime.

Did you know?

1 in 2 women with gestational diabetes get type 2 diabetes within 5 years of delivery? Women with gestational diabetes are at 10 times more likely to develop type 2 diabetes, which requires lifelong treatment.

If you manage your diabetes well during pregnancy, you still have the responsibility to continue healthy lifestyle habits after delivery to prevent transitioning your temporary diabetes into a permanent one.
