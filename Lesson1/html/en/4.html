What causes gestational diabetes?

In all types of diabetes, your body experiences something called insulin resistance, which is when your body’s cells no longer respond effectively to insulin, preventing sugar from entering body cells and causing its levels to increase.

With or without diabetes, insulin resistance is stronger during pregnancy because of the changes in certain hormone levels. Other pregnancy-related factors that contribute to insulin resistance include increased food intake, decreased exercise, and weight gain.

If a women’s body cannot cope with this increased insulin resistance, she will develop gestational diabetes.

There are some factors that can put women at higher risk for gestational diabetes:

  • History of gestational diabetes
  • Greater than 25 years of age
  • Starting pregnancy at a higher weight
  • Too much weight gain during pregnancy- especially in the first 20 weeks.
