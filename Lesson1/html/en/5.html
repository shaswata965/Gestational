Diagnosis

Gestational diabetes could be diagnosed at any time during pregnancy. Because it does not have any symptoms, the only way to diagnose it is through screening tests done by your doctor.

The screening process will usually include screening early in pregnancy with fasting blood tests. If the first test is normal, then you will be asked to do a glucose challenge test. In this test, you need to fast for 8 hours and a fasting blood sample will be withdrawn. Then, you will be asked to have a “glucose drink” and then blood samples will be withdrawn in 1 and 2 hours.
