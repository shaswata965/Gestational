Fasting During Ramadan

All pregnant women with gestational diabetes or a pre-existing diabetes condition who take insulin therapy or metformin are at a high risk for developing complications and should be strongly advised not to fast during Ramadan.

Fasting can affect you in many ways:

You need to consume certain amounts of carbohydrates and calories on daily bases in order to have enough fuel for you and your growing baby. To avoid spikes in your sugar levels, the total calorie intake is usually divided into three big meals and three snacks. Fasting will either result in you getting less calories than you need, or you will end up consuming all the calories over short period of time leading to high blood sugar levels.

Skipping meals for long time (6-8 hours) will use all your sugar or glucose stores in the liver and your body will have to break down fat for fuel. The breakdown of fat will result in the production of acidic bodies called “ketones”. Ketone bodies cross the placenta freely and go to the baby. Studies in animals show that the ketones accumulate in the developing brain and can cause learning difficulties in the future. We are not yet sure if the same thing happens in humans.

Also, too many ketones will increase the acidity in your blood and can make you very sick.

Fasting can also increase the risk of low blood sugar. This could be very severe in some people and can lead to loss of consciousness.

If you want to fast, it will be under your responsibility, you will put yourself and your baby at risk. If you are insisting to fast, you need to know the following:

There are not any plans available to ensure safe fasting.

You should receive education and counseling about your diabetes care during Ramadan.

You should receive close supervision from your diabetes team

Talk to your doctor ahead of time about any changes in your self-monitoring blood sugar and medication plan for Ramadan

It’s strongly recommended that you break your fast if you are hypoglycemic or hyperglycemic, or your medical condition worsens.
