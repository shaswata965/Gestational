Managing Morning Sickness

Nausea and vomiting in pregnancy, commonly known as morning sickness, is very common in early pregnancy. Here are some ways to handle morning illness while dealing with diabetes:

  • If you take insulin, make sure you've got a plan to deal with low blood sugar, as throwing up can make your sugar level drop. Talk to your doctor if you're not sure what to do.
  • Take your time to get yourself out of bed in the morning and at any time.
  • Avoid the types of food that can make you nauseated after eating, like caffeinated, fatty, spicy and fried foods.
  • Beat your nausea with some sugar free peppermint candies, crackers or salty biscuits.
  • Eat smaller portions often and make sure you get enough protein and carbohydrates in every meal.
  • For nausea, drink plenty of water, anise, mint or raspberry leaves boiled in a cup of water.
