Drive Safe with Diabetes

You may not be aware every time your blood sugar levels change, and this can make something as regular as driving, dangerous. To be on the safe side, follow these rules to ensure safe driving:

  • Check your blood sugar levels before driving and do not start the engine unless your blood sugar is above 95 mg/dl or 5.3 mmol/l
  • Avoid long journeys or take breaks in between long rides
  • Always keep a small snack in case your blood sugar drops too low, like a small piece of fruit or wheat biscuits
  • If you feel your blood sugar dropping while driving, immediately pull over and stop the car. Do not continue driving until your blood sugar levels become above 95 mg/dl (5.3 mmol/l) and stay stable for 45 minutes.
  • Always keep a snack and some fast acting sugar in the car to use as needed.
