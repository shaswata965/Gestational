How to Handle a Sick Day

Illness, especially infections, can raise your blood sugar levels. In the case of sick days, this requires you to adjust the frequency of your monitoring plan. Medication adjustments may also be required, but this should be discussed with your doctor.

Check Blood Sugar Levels

  • In case of illness, track your sugar levels every 4 hours.
  • Chat with your health coach for advice if your blood sugar levels are constantly out of target during times of illness.

    • Keep Eating

  • On a sick-day, your appetite might be affected. If you cannot eat your regular meals, get your nutrients from liquids and soft foods.
  • If you are on insulin, continue eating small amounts of carbohydrates even if you aren’t feeling well.
  • Nibble on crackers even before getting out of bed.
  • Make sure to rest, because fatigue may worsen your nausea and vomiting
  • Make substitutions for your usual food if nausea and vomiting make it difficult to eat.

    • Stay Hydrated

  • On sick days, it is important to stay hydrated. Hyperglycemia can result in increased urination and a fever may cause sweating. If you are vomiting or have diarrhea, even more fluids are being lost.
  • Drink water half an hour before or after meals to reduce stomach volume.
  • Caffeine can make you urinate more often, so it is best to avoid coffee and other drinks with caffeine.

    • Continue to Take Medication
    (According to doctor’s orders)

  • Do NOT stop taking your diabetes medication on sick days without consulting your doctor. This is especially true on a sick day, because if you aren’t eating, your blood sugar level rises when your body is stressed by an illness.
  • Some types of illness can cause you to lose your appetite. Normally, insulin without food will lead to a drop in your blood sugar levels. However, because illnesses can raise your blood sugar levels, you should take insulin on a sick day even if you are not eating.

    • Important! Do not take any over-the-counter medications without consulting your doctor. They can affect your blood sugar levels and your baby’s health.
