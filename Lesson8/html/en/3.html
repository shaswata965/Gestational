Oral Medications: Metformin

If diet and exercise are not enough to control your blood sugar levels, your doctor may prescribe a medication called Metformin.

Metformin is a medication that has been used for over 50 years to treat and prevent diabetes.

Metformin works in three ways:

  1. It reduces the amount of sugar produced by your liver;
  2. It reduces the amount of sugar absorbed from food through your stomach;
  3. It makes the insulin that your body produces, work better to reduce the amount of sugar already in your blood.

These three effects will reduce the amount of sugar that reaches the baby and is intended to prevent the baby from putting on too much weight.

Advantages of Metformin

  • Acts as an alternative treatment to insulin
  • May lower the dose of insulin needed if insulin is required
  • Reduces weight gain during pregnancy
  • Can reduce the risk of pre-eclampsia (a severe complication of high blood pressure)

Side effects

In about a third of people, Metformin can cause side effects, which include, nausea, abdominal bloating and flatulence or, less commonly, diarrhea. If you experience extreme fatigue, muscle pain or vomiting you should contact your doctor to discuss these symptoms.

Most women tolerate Metformin very well, particularly if the dose is increased gradually. To reduce side effects, the tablets should be taken in the middle of a meal or straight after a meal. Take each dose with a full glass of water. Side effects usually settle after 1-2 weeks.
