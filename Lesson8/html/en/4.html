Insulin Therapy

Your doctor may prescribe insulin to help keep your blood sugar levels in a healthy range. If you are on Metformin, insulin will be added to it and not replacing it. Using insulin while pregnant is safe.

How does Insulin work?

There are many types of insulin, and they work in different ways after they are injected.

Insulin first starts to lower the glucose levels after it’s injected, and this is referred to as the onset of action. The point in time when insulin lowers the glucose the most is called the peak. The period when the insulin is lowering the glucose is called the duration of action. Insulin types are classified according to their onset, peak, and duration.

Advantages of Insulin

  • Does not put pressure on pancreas to produce insulin
  • Reduces symptoms of high blood sugar
  • Reduces risk of developing diabetes-related complications
  • Gives flexibility of managing blood sugars

For safety reasons, it's important to know when your dose of insulin is working to lower your blood glucose.

Side effects

If you start taking insulin, you can experience side effects of insulin like low blood sugar, weight gain, and skin reactions to injection like redness and swelling.
