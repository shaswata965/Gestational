How to store and dispose insulin, needles and syringes
  • Unopened insulin pens or vials should be kept refrigerated between 2-8 degrees Celsius and protected from light.
  • Even if unopened and unused, insulin kept at room temperature that does not exceed (30°C) degrees Celsius, must be discarded after one month
  • Any opened vials or pen devices of insulin should be stored at room temperature, not to exceed (30°C). After 30 days, they should be discarded, even if some insulin is left in the vial.
  • Used needles are a health hazard. They should be stored in sharp boxes; a container that is shatterproof and has a lid that holds firm if the container is dropped. This sharp box can be disposed at the nearest health center.
  • Storing insulin in the desert: Keep it inside the wet sand if the ice box or refrigerator not available.
