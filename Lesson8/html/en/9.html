Site Rotation

Injecting into the same area for too long can develop scar tissue. Different areas of the body absorb medicines differently. It is not recommended to change randomly from area to area for injection every day. This might lead to getting a different response from your medication each time.

How to do it?

Try to inject into a slightly different spot within an area for up to 2 weeks before switching to another area. For example, inject into the arms slightly into different spots each time for up to two weeks. Then rotate to the thighs, using slightly different spots for the next 2 weeks. If you choose to inject into the abdomen, you do not need to rotate to other areas of the body as the abdomen is large enough to simply rotate your injection sites within this area.
