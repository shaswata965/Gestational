Preparing and Taking an Injection Using a Needle

If it’s your first time using a needle injection, it’s important to follow these rules to avoid any complications:

Never inject into the muscle as it can cause medications to be absorbed too quickly

Avoid injecting into any scars

Don't inject 2 inches around the belly button

Never inject into a vein as this is very dangerous

To make sure you don't inject into the muscle, pinch up the fat between your fingers and insert the needle at a 90 degrees angle.

Please note: For 4 mm and 5 mm long needles, no skin fold is required for pinching, just inject straight in

For 6-8mm and 12.7 mm long needles, a skin fold is recommended to reduce the risk of injecting into the muscle.

You may see a small blood drop on the skin just after the injection, or you may bruise later on. This is common and nothing to worry about.

Never reuse the same needle for an inject. Always use a new one. At the end, please discard used needle in a shatter-proof sharp box.
