Follow these Rules!

While exercise is strongly encouraged for pregnant woman, there are a few types of exercises to avoid in order to prevent any harm to the baby or you:

Rule 1: Don’t lie flat on your back.

The weight of your “baby bump” presses on the big blood vessels and can make you feel faint and reduce blood flow to your baby.

Rule 2: Don’t take part in contact sports.

Especially where there’s a risk of being hit, such as kickboxing, squash, tennis, football.

Rule 3: Don’t take part in exercises that involve bouncing motions.

This includes horse riding, downhill skiing, ice skiing water skiing, soccer, outdoor cycling, basketball, most racquet sports, and scuba diving, because there’s a risk of falling.

Rule 4: Don’t push yourself too much.

Stop exercising if you feel any of the these:

Excessive fatigue or shortness of breath

Pain or cramping, especially in the back or pelvic area

Vaginal bleeding

Dizziness

Palpitations or unusual sensations in your chest

Persistent contractions
