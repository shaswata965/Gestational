Being Active

If you do not have any medical or pregnancy-related complications, it’s recommended that you do at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise a day on most, if not all, days of the week (or 150 minutes per week).

Brisk walking is one of the best activities you can do, because of low risk for injury. Brisk walking means walking faster than your normal pace.

→ Did you know…?

Being active for 10 minutes at a time three times a day is just as effective as a single 30 minutes session? You can do this by taking a brisk walk for ten minutes after each meal.

If you were routinely exercising before pregnancy you can continue to do so during pregnancy as well, if you are healthy and receive proper medical care.

Important: Before starting any physical activity program, make sure to consult with your doctor. If you were not very active before being pregnant, please don’t try to start a high-intensity workout program during pregnancy.
