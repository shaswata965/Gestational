Preeclampsia

Gestational diabetes places pregnant woman at a higher risk for developing disorders involving high blood pressure, like preeclampsia.

Preeclampsia is a serious complication diagnosed after the 20th week in pregnancy. It can occur both during pregnancy or after delivery.

Symptoms of preeclampsia:

  • High blood pressure
  • Edema (swelling)
  • Proteinuria (high levels of protein found in urine)

Preeclampsia can go unnoticed in pregnant women, because no one can feel their blood pressure going up. Make sure to check your blood pressure at every visit to your doctor. If your blood pressure is greater than 140/90, then you should speak with your doctor about your high blood pressure.

Preeclampsia can be prevented by:

  • Managing blood sugar levels
  • Skipping the added salt to your meals
  • Drinking 8 glasses of water daily
  • Avoiding fast food
  • Staying active regularly
  • Elevating your feet several times during the day
  • Avoiding alcohol and caffeine
