Extra Precautions
  • After treating hypoglycemia, check your blood sugar levels regularly over the next hour to see if it went back down again.
  • Do not keep eating after the initial treatment, wait 15- 20 minutes and then test blood sugar levels to determine whether further treatment is needed
  • Do NOT keep eating until symptoms disappear
  • Avoid using high fat foods for treating low blood sugar like chocolate and milk. It takes your body a longer time to break down the fats and use the carbohydrates.
  • Keep track of your blood sugar readings to help you spot trends of high and low blood sugar readings.
  • Always carry a carbohydrate source like glucose tablets on hand and at your bedside in case of experiencing low blood sugar during the night
  • Keep a carbohydrate source, in the car at all times to treat episodes of hypoglycemia that might occur while driving
  • Wear a diabetes identification card
