Hypoglycemia

Hypoglycemia is when your blood glucose sugar level drops to a level that is below normal (< 70 mg/dl). During pregnancy, how your body reacts to insulin can make your blood sugar drop low.

Women experience hypoglycemia more often in the first trimester when insulin resistance is at its lowest.

Causes

  • Delaying or skipping snacks
  • Taking too much diabetes medication or taking it at the wrong time
  • Doing more physical activity than usual

Symptoms

  • Shaking/trembling/sweating
  • Dizziness/lightheadedness/nausea
  • Sleepiness
  • Nervous feeling
  • Trouble concentrating
  • Intense hunger
  • headache
  • Blurred vision
  • Fast pulse
  • Heart palpitations
  • Tingling in hands, feet, lips
