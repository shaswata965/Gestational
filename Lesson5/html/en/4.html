Choosing the Right Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are divided into two groups based on what is known as glycemic index (GI). High GI foods are broken down quickly and the glucose enters the blood stream at a fast rate leading to a spike in blood sugar levels.

Low glycemic index (GI) food are carbohydrates that your body takes longer to break. The longer it takes to breakdown the food, the slower the raise in blood sugar levels, the fuller and less hungry you will feel.

Examples of Low GI food are:

Most non-starchy vegetables, such as broccoli, spinach, carrots, cauliflower, zucchini

Whole-wheat bread, pasta, and cereals.

Beans, peas and lentils

Most Fruits

Milk

Examples of high GI food are:

White bread

Corn flakes, puffed rice, bran flakes, instant oatmeal

Short grain white rice, rice pasta, macaroni and cheese

French fries

Juice and soft drinks

Table sugar

All desserts such as cakes, doughnuts, chocolate and ice cream

Melons and pineapple

Dates

Dairy products like milk and yogurt are a natural source of carbohydrates. Choose lower fat options free of added sugars and keep your portions limited.
