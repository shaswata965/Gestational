Let’s Review!
  1. Have three meals and two snacks.

    Spreading out your food intake over the course of the day can prevent major spikes in blood sugar levels.

  2. Limit your fruit and starchy vegetable portions.

    They are high in natural sugars and can spike your blood sugar levels

  3. Avoid added sugars.

    Skip out on sugar, honey, and other syrups

  4. Avoid fruit juices.

    These can cause your blood sugar to rise dramatically, which isn’t good for you and the baby.

  5. Don’t skip breakfast!

    Make sure to include a lean source of protein in the morning and avoid sugary cereals.

  6. Eat less high-sugar foods.

    You should strictly limit cake, pastries, cookies, baklava… and all desserts.
