Lower Salt Intake

Although sodium is not its own category as a food group, it is still very important to monitor your sodium intake.

Table salt is made up of sodium and chlorine. Too much sodium in your diet can cause your body to retain too much fluids, this can raise your blood pressure and put an extra strain on your heart. Gradually decreasing the amount of sodium in your diet can help control your blood pressure.

Try some of these tips to reduce your salt intake:

Take the salt-shaker off the dinner table

Reduce or eliminate added salt or sodium while cooking

Rinse and drain canned vegetables before cooking them

Choose fresh foods over canned or processed food

Limit salty snacks
