How to Read a Food Label

Start by looking at the top of the food label, where you will find the serving size and the number of servings per container.

Compare your portion size (the amount you actually eat) to the serving size listed on the label. For example, if you were planning on eating 4 biscuits from a container, but the serving size listed on the package is 2, you should take that into consideration when looking at the nutrition fact.

Check out the total calories. This could be tricky at times. For example, a bag of salted peanut states that there are only 100 calories per serving (30g). That doesnâ€™t mean the whole bag is 100 calories. The food label says the bag contains a total of 90 grams, that means if you eat the whole bag you will have 300 calories!

Check which nutrients are high or low in a serving of food by looking at the % on the label. The higher the percentage, the more of that ingredient is in the food.

The most important part of the label is the amount of carbohydrates in 1 serving.

Check the ingredients list at the bottom: Ingredients are listed by weight, in the order of quantity, from the largest ingredient amount to the smallest. People with food allergies should always pay close attention to the ingredient list!
